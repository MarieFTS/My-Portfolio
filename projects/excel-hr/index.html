HR Analytics & Reporting

Built a comprehensive Excel-based HR analytics system tracking headcount, PTO balances, and team engagement metrics at Jamela Hair. Data was normalized and visualized in Tableau for management reporting.

Oct 2022 – Jan 2023 Jamela Hair Excel + Tableau 5 Workbook Sheets

Overview

This HR analytics system transformed manual people management at Jamela Hair into a data-driven process. By creating structured Excel templates and automating key calculations, we reduced administrative overhead by 3 hours per week while gaining visibility into turnover trends and engagement levels. The normalized dataset enabled Tableau dashboards that helped leadership identify retention risks and celebrate team engagement improvements.

Process & Methodology

1

Employee Master Sheet

Built structured employee table with ID column, status, department, hire date for XLOOKUP-ready data.

2

Headcount Tracker

Created dynamic monthly headcount using COUNTIFS across departments and status filters.

3

PTO Log & Balance

Tracked requests and approvals using NETWORKDAYS for working days and VLOOKUP for allocations.

4

Engagement Metrics

Captured monthly 5-dimension survey scores with AVERAGEIF aggregation by department.

5

Dashboard Sheet

Built auto-updating KPI dashboard with conditional formatting, sparklines, and trending indicators.

6

Tableau Integration

Exported normalized data to Tableau for interactive headcount charts and engagement analysis.

Excel Formula Examples

Excel 
-- Active headcount per department
=COUNTIFS(MasterData[Status],"Active",
          MasterData[Department],A2)

-- Working days (excluding weekends)
=NETWORKDAYS(D2, E2) - 1

-- Remaining PTO balance
=VLOOKUP(A2, PTOAllocation[#All], 3, FALSE)
 - SUMIF(PTOLog[Employee_ID], A2,
         PTOLog[Days_Used])

-- Month-over-month % change
=IFERROR((C2-B2)/B2, 0)
